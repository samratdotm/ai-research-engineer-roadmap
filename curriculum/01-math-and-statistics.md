# Phase 1 - Math and Statistics

## Scope

Learn the subset of mathematics used to understand training, metrics, and experimental uncertainty. Do not attempt to complete entire university courses.

## Required concepts

### Linear algebra

- Vectors, matrices, tensors, dimensions, shapes, transpose, inverse, and broadcasting.
- Linear combinations, basis intuition, dot products, matrix multiplication, norms, cosine similarity.
- Orthogonality, projections, least squares, eigenvalues, and SVD at an intuitive level.

### Calculus and optimization

- Functions, derivatives, partial derivatives, gradients, chain rule, and Jacobian intuition.
- Loss surfaces, gradient descent, learning rates, local minima, saddle points, and numerical stability.

### Probability and statistics

- Events, conditional probability, Bayes' rule, random variables, distributions, expectation, and variance.
- Covariance, correlation, sampling, estimators, bias, standard error, confidence intervals, and hypothesis tests.
- Why repeated runs and uncertainty matter in ML experiments.

## Practice standard

For every mathematical object, provide:

1. a plain-language meaning;
2. its shape or units;
3. a NumPy implementation;
4. one ML use;
5. one common mistake.

## Exit questions

- Why does matrix multiplication appear in a neural layer?
- How does the chain rule make backpropagation possible?
- Why is a one-seed improvement not conclusive?
- What does a 95% confidence interval mean, and what does it not mean?
